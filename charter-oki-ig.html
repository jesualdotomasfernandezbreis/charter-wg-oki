DRAFT Ontologies and Knowledge Graphs in Industry Interest Group Charter

The mission of the Ontologies and Knowledge Graphs in Industry Interest Group is to enable seamless integration of standards-based semantic assets into web-based information systems, making it easier to discover, link, and reuse industrial data across platforms.

Motivation and Background

Today’s industries—whether in energy, aerospace, manufacturing, or other sectors—rely on large amounts of data to keep things running smoothly. This data describes everything from equipment and locations to services and business functions. To keep that information organized and understandable, different sectors use different systems and standards, like ISO 81346 or CFIHOS.
However, these systems don’t always speak the same "language". Therefore, when companies or departments try to share information, mismatches between standards can cause confusion, mistakes, and wasted time. The limited ability to manage, reuse, and repurpose data introduces friction, increases costs, and reduces competitiveness. This creates problems not just for everyday operations, but also for big-picture goals like automation, data reuse, and using AI. Poorly aligned data makes it harder to innovate and stay competitive.
To solve this, we need a shared, transparent way to describe and manage these systems—one that works for both people and computers. By turning the standards into structured vocabularies and ontologies, we can ensure smoother communication, better data quality, and more powerful digital tools across industries. This unified approach is essential for the future of industry in the digital age.

Scope

We propose to establish a unified and transparent framework for the development, implementation, and maintenance of industrial designation systems and standards as ontologies—specifically focusing on ISO 81346.
Our aim is to foster a shared understanding of how such standards are applied within industry information systems, particularly for classifying and profiling equipment, functions, locations and services.
This Interest Group would serve as a governance hub for these assets, including ontologies and controlled vocabularies, and would define a methodology for their lifecycle management.
The group will also provide guidance on registration practices, data catalog integration, and long-term stewardship of these semantic assets.

Out of Scope

The following features are out of scope, and will not be addressed by this Interest Group:

  • Development of new industrial standards beyond those already established (e.g., the group will focus on representing existing standards like ISO 81346, not creating new ones).

  • Direct implementation or customization of ontologies within specific commercial software products (the group may provide guidance, but not act as a deployment team).

  • Management of proprietary or confidential data—the focus is on open, shareable semantic assets and methodologies.

  • Certification or compliance auditing related to standard implementation; the group provides frameworks, not formal certification services.

  • Ontology development unrelated to designation systems (e.g., product lifecycle ontologies, materials ontologies, etc., unless they intersect directly with ISO 81346 concepts).

  • General-purpose web ontology governance (the group's scope is specific to industrial designation systems and related semantic assets).

    Tentative Deliverables

    • Description of relevant industry use cases, as well as requirement documents and quality and acceptance criteria.
    • Design of the architecture of a common data model that will enable interoperability across industries through ontologies and knowledge graphs.
    • Design of a network of ontologies that will supported the Common Data Model.
    • Test suite and implementation report for the specification.
    • Primer or Best Practice documents to support web developers when designing applications.

    Success Criteria


    • Community Engagement and Participation
      • Establish a diverse and active membership with representatives from the relevant stakeholder groups (e.g., industry, SDOs, ontology engineers, software vendors, academia).
      • Hold regular meetings with documented minutes and increasing participation over time.
      • Engage with other W3C groups, to ensure cross-pollination.
    • Standards Alignment and Interoperability
      • Publish a position paper or white paper outlining the application of ISO 81346 as an ontology and its relationship to existing W3C standards.
      • Demonstrate interoperability through at least one working prototype or case study where the proposed ontologies are used in an existing industrial information system.
    • Outreach and Knowledge Dissemination
      • Organize at least one public workshop or webinar per year, involving standard bodies (e.g., ISO, IEC), industry users, and researchers.
    • Sustainability and Long-Term Impact
      • Identify and collaborate with at least one standards development organization (SDO) to align methodologies and promote adoption.
      • Establish a roadmap for post-interest group activities, potentially leading to a formal W3C Working Group if appropriate.

    Participation

    To be successful, this Interest Group is expected to have 6 or more active participants for its duration, including representatives from industry, academia, and standardization bodies. The Chairs are expected to contribute a quarter of a working day per week towards the Interest Group. There is no minimum requirement for other Participants.

    The group encourages questions, comments and issues on its public mailing lists and document repositories, as described in Communication. The group also welcomes non-Members to contribute through its public communication channels.

    Participants in the group are required (by the W3C Process) to follow the W3C Code of Conduct.

    Communication

    Discussions for this Interest Group are conducted in public: the meeting minutes from teleconference and face-to-face meetings will be archived for public review, and technical discussions and issue tracking will be conducted in a manner that can be both read and written to by the general public. The meetings themselves are not open to public participation, however.

    Information about the group (including details about deliverables, issues, actions, status, participants, and meetings) will be available from the Ontologies and Knowledge Graphs in Industry Interest Group home page.

    Most Ontologies and Knowledge Graphs in Industry Interest Group teleconferences will focus on discussion of particular specifications, and will be conducted on an as-needed basis.

    This group primarily conducts its technical work on the public mailing list public-oki@w3.org (archive) or on GitHub issues. The public is invited to review, discuss and contribute to this work.

    The group may use a Member-confidential mailing list for administrative purposes and, at the discretion of the Chairs and members of the group, for member-only discussions in special cases when a participant requests such a discussion.

    Decision Policy

    This group will seek to make decisions through consensus and due process, per the W3C Process Document (section 5.2.1, Consensus).

    As this group is exploratory, the group will:

    1. gather information, ensuring that all sides of controversial ideas are considered,
    2. seek consensus on matters of fact, and if possible on matters of prediction/prescription,
    3. publish reports recording the various positions, and may record the rough division of opinions in the group across those positions.

    To afford asynchronous decisions and organizational deliberation, any resolution taken in a face-to-face meeting or teleconference will be considered provisional. Group participants have a one week period to comment on provisional resolutions.

    All decisions made by the group should be considered resolved unless and until new information becomes available or unless reopened at the discretion of the Chairs.

    This charter is written in accordance with the W3C Process Document (Section 5.2.3, Deciding by Vote) and includes no voting procedures beyond what the Process Document requires.
